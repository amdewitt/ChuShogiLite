Historic Chu Shogi Puzzles

This is a collection of 250 Chu Shogi Puzzles. The first 210 of these (Series A, B, C, and D, as well as E1 through E10) are a subset of 224 puzzles from the 1992 Middle Shogi Manual (MSM) by George Hodges. The SFENs were provided by H. G. Muller. The rest were sourced from other places. E11 and E12 were made by the applet's author, A. M. DeWitt, specifically for this collection. E13 was sourced from the Shinan collection and was the only puzzle from that collection that was not also in the MSM. The rest were sourced from the TumeChu collection. All solutions and repairs were verified with HaChu, Muller's Chu Shogi engine.

The puzzles are tsume shogi problems, which are forced mate problems where the attacker must constantly check the enemy King. They are split into 4 series of 50 problems each (not counting different versions of the same problem) according to how they were in the original source material, with one exception. 14 of the problems in the MSM's original D-series were left out due to being difficult to repair or impossible. 50 of the remaining problems were renamed to account for this, 26 were moved to the A-series (A25-A50), and the rest were moved to the E-series (E1-E10).
